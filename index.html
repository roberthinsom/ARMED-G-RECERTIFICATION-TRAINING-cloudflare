Understanding the Armed G License: What It Entails

An Armed G license allows security officers to carry firearms while on duty, typically in the state of Florida. Obtaining this license involves initial training, including firearms safety, legal education, and hands-on practice. However, maintaining this certification requires regular recertification to ensure the armed security officer remains knowledgeable and skilled.

Legal and Professional Requirements for Recertification

Meeting State Regulations

Most states, including Florida, mandate that security professionals with an Armed G license complete recertification training every year. This process includes requalification on the firearm range, updates on legal changes, and refreshed training on best practices for safety and security.

Maintaining Professional Standards

Beyond legal requirements, recertification is a mark of professionalism. It signals to employers and clients that a security officer is committed to maintaining their skills and upholding the highest standards in the field.

Key Components of Armed G Recertification Training

  • Firearm Proficiency and Safety: Live-fire exercises and shooting drills under timed conditions.
  • Legal Updates and Compliance: Staying updated on changes in laws governing the use of force and firearm regulations.
  • Tactical and Situational Training: Scenario-based training including de-escalation techniques and crisis management.

The Risks of Letting Your Certification Lapse

Failing to complete recertification on time can lead to the suspension of the Armed G license. This can result in job loss, legal liabilities, and a diminished reputation within the security industry.
